## CPB 0660 — Knee Arthroplasty (Total Knee Replacement)

**Source:** https://www.aetna.com/cpb/medical/data/600_699/0660.html

### Medical Necessity Criteria — Total Knee Arthroplasty (TKA)

An FDA-approved TKA prosthesis is considered **medically necessary** for adult members when **all** of the following are met:

1. **Advanced joint disease** demonstrated by:
   - Pain and functional disability that interferes with ADLs from injury due to osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, avascular necrosis, or post-traumatic arthritis of the knee joint
   - Limited range of motion, crepitus, or effusion/swelling of knee joint on physical examination
2. **Radiographic evidence** of one of:
   - Moderate/severe osteoarthritis (Kellgren-Lawrence Grade 3 or 4)
   - Avascular necrosis (osteonecrosis) of tibial or femoral condyle
   - Rheumatoid arthritis (joint space narrowing)
3. **History of unsuccessful conservative therapy** (non-surgical medical management):
   - **12 weeks** standard requirement
   - **24 weeks** for persons with a relative contraindication (see below)
   - At least half of the conservative therapy must be **formal in-person physical therapy** with a licensed physical therapist (not home or virtual PT) within the past year

### Conservative Therapy Components

Non-surgical management includes: patient education, active physical therapy, NSAIDs/acetaminophen, weight management counseling, activity modification, bracing, and corticosteroid or viscosupplementation injections as appropriate.

### Relative Contraindications (Triggering 24-Week Requirement)

- Morbid obesity (BMI > 40)
- Age < 50 years
- Members with relative contraindications should exhaust all nonsurgical treatment options

### Absolute Contraindications (Not Medically Necessary)

TKA is considered **not medically necessary** in persons with any of the following:

- Active infection of the joint or active systemic bacteremia not totally eradicated
- Active skin infection or open wound within the planned surgical site (exception: recurrent cutaneous staph infections)
- **Corticosteroid injection into the joint within 12 weeks** of the planned arthroplasty
- Vascular insufficiency, significant muscular atrophy of the leg, or neuromuscular disease severe enough to compromise implant stability or post-operative recovery, or quadriplegia
- Osseous abnormalities that cannot be optimally managed and would increase likelihood of poor surgical outcome (inadequate bone stock)
- Allergy to components of the implant (cobalt, chromium, alumina)

### Unicompartmental Knee Arthroplasty (UKA)

Considered **medically necessary** using FDA-approved devices when:

- Advanced osteoarthritis or post-traumatic arthritis affecting **only a single compartment**
- Pain/functional disability interfering with ADLs
- Limited range of motion or physical findings
- Intact stable ligaments (particularly ACL)
- Knee arc of motion not limited to 90 degrees or less
- Radiographic evidence of moderate/severe OA (Kellgren-Lawrence Grade 3 or 4) affecting only a single compartment

### Experimental / Investigational

- Bicompartmental and bi-unicompartmental knee arthroplasty
- Custom instrumentation for knee arthroplasty
- Computer-assisted navigation (MAKOplasty)
- Augmented/virtual reality assistance for knee arthroplasty

### CPT Codes

| 27446 | Unicompartmental (partial) knee arthroplasty |
| 27447 | Total knee arthroplasty |
| 27486 | Revision TKA — 1 component |
| 27487 | Revision TKA — femoral and entire tibial component |

## CPB 0673 — Knee Arthroscopy / Osteoarthritis of the Knee

**Source:** https://www.aetna.com/cpb/medical/data/600_699/0673.html
**Note:** The user-requested CPB 0233 does not appear to exist as a knee surgery CPB. CPB 0673 is Aetna's current CPB for knee arthroscopy, meniscectomy, and ACL reconstruction.

### Medical Necessity Criteria — Arthroscopic Knee Surgery

Arthroscopic knee surgery (with or without partial meniscectomy or meniscal repair) is considered **medically necessary** when:

1. Significant knee pain **plus mechanical symptoms**
2. No more than mild osteoarthritis (Kellgren-Lawrence 0, 1, or 2; or modified Outerbridge Grade 0, 1, or 2)
3. **Radiologic confirmation** of the pathology
4. **Failure of conservative therapy** — minimum **6 weeks of formal in-person physical therapy** in the past year

### Meniscectomy / Meniscal Repair

**Arthroscopic partial meniscectomy or meniscal repair** is considered medically necessary for:

- **Traumatic meniscal tears** with significant symptoms
- No more than mild OA (Kellgren-Lawrence 0, 1, or 2)
- **MRI confirmation** of the meniscal pathology
- Failure of conservative therapy (6 weeks formal PT)
- **Exception:** Conservative treatment requirement is waived if the knee is **"locked" due to a displaced bucket handle tear**

**Meniscal root tear repair** is medically necessary when:

- Significant symptoms present
- No more than mild OA (Kellgren-Lawrence 0, 1, or 2; or modified Outerbridge Grade 0, 1, or 2)
- Adequate vascular supply to the torn portion
- Meniscal tissue is not degenerated on imaging

### Not Medically Necessary / Experimental

- **Arthroscopic partial meniscectomy for degenerative meniscal tears** — experimental and investigational
- **Arthroscopic lavage** — experimental and investigational (effectiveness not established)
- **Arthroscopic debridement for knee pain only or severe OA** — experimental and investigational
- Arthroscopic debridement **may** be medically necessary for mild-to-moderate OA with mechanical problems (loose bodies, meniscal tears)

### Integral Procedures (Not Separately Billable)

- Minor synovectomy (CPT 29875) — integral to all other arthroscopic knee procedures
- Chondroplasty/debridement (CPT 29877) — integral to all other arthroscopic knee procedures

### CPT Codes

| Code | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 29880 | Meniscectomy — medial AND lateral |
| 29881 | Meniscectomy — medial OR lateral |
| 29882 | Meniscus repair — medial OR lateral |
| 29883 | Meniscus repair — medial AND lateral |
| 29888 | ACL repair/augmentation/reconstruction (arthroscopically aided) |
| 29889 | Posterior cruciate ligament repair/augmentation/reconstruction |

## CPB 0837 — Shoulder Arthroplasty and Arthrodesis (including Rotator Cuff)

**Source:** https://www.aetna.com/cpb/medical/data/800_899/0837.html
**Note:** The user-requested CPB 0626 is actually "Hematopoietic Cell Transplantation for Thalassemia Major and Sickle Cell Anemia" — not rotator cuff. CPB 0837 is Aetna's CPB covering shoulder arthroplasty, and rotator cuff repair is typically covered under standard surgical benefits with CPT 29827.

### Medical Necessity Criteria — Shoulder Arthroplasty

Shoulder arthroplasty is considered **medically necessary** when:

1. Pain and functional disability of **at least 6 months duration** that interferes with ADLs
2. History of **unsuccessful conservative therapy** (non-surgical medical management) clearly documented in the medical record
3. If conservative therapy is not appropriate, the medical record must clearly document **why** such approach is not reasonable

### Conservative Therapy Waiver

A trial of conservative therapy is **not required** for:

- Fractures
- Reconstruction following tumor resection
- Glenoid bone loss with anterior or posterior subluxation
- Avascular necrosis of the humeral head with collapse in the presence of severe OA of the shoulder

### Covered Indications

- Deficient rotator cuff with glenohumeral arthropathy (reverse total shoulder arthroplasty)
- Severe glenohumeral arthritis
- Failed prior shoulder arthroplasty (revision)
- Fractures of the proximal humerus

### Imaging

- Three-dimensional pre-operative imaging for total shoulder arthroplasty is a covered service

### Rotator Cuff Repair (CPT 29827)

Rotator cuff repair via arthroscopy (CPT 29827) is generally covered as a standard surgical benefit. Medical necessity requires:

- Imaging-confirmed tear (MRI or arthrogram)
- Functional limitation documented
- Documented course of conservative care (unless acute, repairable full-thickness tear with compelling timing)

## CPB 0743 — Spinal Surgery: Laminectomy and Fusion

**Source:** https://www.aetna.com/cpb/medical/data/700_799/0743.html

### Medical Necessity Criteria — Cervical Laminectomy / Discectomy / Fusion

Cervical laminectomy (and/or anterior and/or posterior cervical discectomy and fusion) is considered **medically necessary** for herniated discs or other causes of spinal cord/nerve root compression when **all of the following** are met:

1. All other reasonable sources of pain and/or neurological deficit have been ruled out
2. Member has **signs or symptoms of neural compression** (radiculopathy, neurogenic claudication, myelopathy) associated with the levels being treated
3. Advanced imaging studies indicate **central/lateral recess or foraminal stenosis** graded as **moderate, moderate-to-severe, or severe** (not mild or mild-to-moderate), or nerve root/spinal cord compression

### Conservative Treatment Requirements

- Minimum **6 weeks** of documented conservative management for lumbar decompression/fusion
- Conservative therapy includes: structured physical therapy (minimum 6 weeks formal in-person therapy), NSAIDs, and consideration of epidural steroid injections
- At least **3 months** of documented conservative management for some indications

### Medically Necessary Indications for Pedicle Screw Fixation

Pedicle screws for spinal fixation are considered medically necessary for:

- Fusion adjacent to prior lumbar fusion
- Fusion after decompression
- Pseudoarthrosis repair
- Revision lumbar disc surgery requiring instrumentation because of instability at the previous level
- Scoliosis and kyphosis requiring spinal instrumentation
- Segmental defects or loss of posterior elements following tumor resection
- Spinal trauma of all types (fractures and dislocations)
- Spondylolisthesis — grades I to IV

### Synthetic Spine Cages/Spacers

Considered medically necessary for cervical fusion involving corpectomy for:

- Fracture
- Tumor
- Central canal stenosis with myelopathy

### Not Medically Necessary

- **Lumbar spinal fusion for degenerative disc disease** and all other indications not explicitly listed as medically necessary
- Presence of spondylolisthesis alone **without documented instability** does not meet fusion criteria
- Radiologic documentation of **at least moderate stenosis** or other radiologic evidence of neural compression is required

### Key CPT Codes

| 22630 | Arthrodesis, posterior interbody technique, lumbar — single interspace |
| 22633 | Arthrodesis, combined posterior interbody with posterolateral — lumbar |
| 22558 | Anterior lumbar interbody fusion — first interspace |
| 22585 | ALIF — each additional interspace |

## CPB 0502 — Nerve Conduction Studies

**Source:** https://www.aetna.com/cpb/medical/data/500_599/0502.html

### Medical Necessity Criteria

NCS (CPT codes 95907-95913) are considered medically necessary to assess the integrity and diagnose diseases of the peripheral nervous system, specifically assessing:

- Speed (conduction velocity and/or latency)
- Size (amplitude)
- Shape of the response
- Pathological findings: conduction slowing, conduction block, no response, low amplitude response

### Frequency Limits

- **2 sessions per year** is considered appropriate for most conditions:
  - Unilateral or bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome
  - Radiculopathy
  - Mononeuropathy
  - Polyneuropathy
  - Myopathy
  - Neuromuscular junction disorders
- Studies performed **more frequently than twice a year** are subject to medical necessity review

### Carpal Tunnel Syndrome — Specific Criteria

When NCS are performed **solely to diagnose or rule out carpal tunnel syndrome**:

1. Sensory conduction studies across the wrist of the median nerve
2. If results are abnormal: one other sensory nerve in the symptomatic limb
3. If the initial median sensory NCS across the wrist has a **conduction distance greater than 8 cm** and results are normal, additional studies required:
   - Comparison of median sensory conduction across the wrist with radial or ulnar sensory conduction across the wrist in the same limb, **or**
   - Median sensory conduction across the wrist over a short (7 to 8 cm) conduction distance

### Not Medically Necessary for Carpal Tunnel

- **F-wave studies** are not necessary for carpal tunnel syndrome (may be used for other conditions evaluating proximal nerve segments)

### Experimental / Investigational / Unproven

- Electrodiagnostic tests for diagnosis of distal symmetric polyneuropathy
- NCS using **automated devices**
- F-wave studies for carpal tunnel syndrome
- Intraoperative NCS during prostate surgery or wrist arthroscopy
- NCS for diagnosis of organophosphorus pesticide exposure
- NCS for evaluation of COVID-19 associated neuromuscular disorders
- NCS for the **sole purpose of monitoring disease intensity or treatment effectiveness** for polyneuropathy of diabetes or end-stage renal disease
- **Surface scanning EMG**, paraspinal surface EMG, and macro EMG — experimental/investigational for low back pain and all other indications

### CPT Codes

| Code | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 95907 | NCS, 1-2 studies |
| 95908 | NCS, 3-4 studies |
| 95909 | NCS, 5-6 studies |
| 95910 | NCS, 7-8 studies |
| 95911 | NCS, 9-10 studies |
| 95912 | NCS, 11-12 studies |
| 95913 | NCS, 13+ studies |

### Documentation Requirements

- Waveforms must be reviewed **on site in real time** with technique adjustments as needed
- Reports prepared **on site by the examiner** with interpretation of test results
- Includes calculation of NCV (sometimes including specialized F-wave indices)
- Comparison to normal values and physician interpretation required

## CPB 0194 — Spinal Cord Stimulation

**Source:** https://www.aetna.com/cpb/medical/data/100_199/0194.html

### Medical Necessity Criteria — Permanent Implantation

Permanent implantation of a dorsal column stimulator (DCS) is considered **medically necessary** when:

1. **Other more conservative methods of pain management have been tried and failed**
2. Member has **chronic pain** due to an approved indication (see below)
3. Member has experienced **significant pain reduction (50% or more)** with a **3- to 7-day trial** of percutaneous spinal stimulation
4. **Psychological evaluation** completed (referenced in criteria)
5. Conservative treatment failure documented for **at least 6 months**

### Trial Requirement

A **trial of percutaneous spinal stimulation** is considered medically necessary for members who meet the above criteria, to predict whether a permanent DCS will induce significant pain relief.

- Trial duration: **3 to 7 days**
- Success threshold: **50% or greater pain reduction**

### Approved Indications

- **Failed back surgery syndrome (FBSS)** — low back pain with significant radicular pain
- **Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS)** Types I and II
- **Diabetic peripheral neuropathy** of the lower extremities
- Intractable low back pain and leg pain
- Radicular pain syndrome
- Arachnoiditis

### Alternative Stimulation Technologies (Equally Effective)

Considered equally effective alternatives to standard DCS:

- High-frequency spinal cord stimulation (Senza / HF10)
- Burst stimulation (BurstDR)
- Differential target multiplexed stimulation (Medtronic DTM)

### Replacement Criteria

Replacement of cervical, lumbar, or thoracic DCS or battery/generator is medically necessary when:

- Member has had a **positive pain relief response** from the existing DCS
- Existing stimulator or battery/generator is **no longer under warranty and cannot be repaired**

### Not Medically Necessary / Experimental / Investigational

- DCS for **headache and occipital neuralgia** — lack of evidence for sustained pain relief
- DCS for **intractable angina** — limited coverage; contraindications presented in appendix
- DCS for indications not listed as approved

### CPT Codes

| Code | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 63650 | Percutaneous implantation of neurostimulator electrode(s), epidural |
| 63685 | Insertion/replacement of spinal neurostimulator pulse generator or receiver |
| 63688 | Revision or removal of implanted spinal neurostimulator pulse generator or receiver |

## CPB 0591 — Intervertebral Disc Prostheses (Artificial Disc Replacement)

**Source:** https://www.aetna.com/cpb/medical/data/500_599/0591.html

### Medical Necessity Criteria — Cervical Disc Arthroplasty

FDA-approved prosthetic intervertebral discs are considered **medically necessary** for treatment of skeletally mature persons with symptomatic cervical degenerative disc disease or herniated disc when **all of the following** are met:

1. All other reasonable sources of pain and/or neurological deficit have been ruled out
2. Member has **signs or symptoms of neural compression** (radiculopathy, neurogenic claudication, myelopathy) associated with the levels being treated
3. Central/lateral recess or foraminal stenosis graded as **moderate, moderate-to-severe, or severe** (not mild or mild-to-moderate)
4. **No radiologic evidence of segmental instability**
5. Member has **failed at least 6 weeks of conservative therapy**

### Conservative Therapy Requirements

Conservative measures must be **recent (within the past year)** and include:

- Patient education
- **Active physical therapy** (in-person, not home or virtual PT)
- Medications (NSAIDs, acetaminophen, or tricyclic antidepressants)
- Where appropriate: identification and management of associated anxiety and depression
- PT participation for the **entire required duration** (6 weeks minimum)

### Device-Specific Age Requirements

| Device | Age Requirement | Additional |
|--------|----------------|------------|
| Bryan Cervical Disc | >= 21 years | No osteoporosis |
| Mobi-C | >= 21 and <= 67 years | — |
| Prestige Cervical Disc | >= 21 and <= 78 years | — |
| Prestige LP | >= 21 and <= 78 years | — |

### Diabetic Members — Additional Requirement

- **HbA1c must be < 8%** within 3 months prior to surgery
- **Exception:** This requirement is waived if there is myelopathy, cauda equina syndrome, severe weakness (MRC scale 4- or less), or progressive weakness

### Lumbar Disc Arthroplasty

- FDA-approved total disc implant (CPT 22857) at **one level** for lumbar degenerative disc disease is now covered (revised 2/8/2023 following class action settlement)
- ICD codes: M51.36-M51.37
- Patient must meet specific criteria as listed in the full policy

### Cervical Levels Covered

- C3 to C7

### Experimental / Investigational / Unproven

- **Lumbar partial disc prosthetics** (Nubac, DASCOR)
- **Lumbar prosthetic intervertebral discs at more than one level**
- MOTUS lumbar total joint replacement implant (3Spine)
- Total posterior spine (TOPS) System

### CPT Codes

| Code | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 22856 | Cervical disc arthroplasty — single level |
| 22858 | Cervical disc arthroplasty — second level |
| 22857 | Lumbar disc arthroplasty — single level |
| 0375T | Total disc arthroplasty, revision — cervical |
