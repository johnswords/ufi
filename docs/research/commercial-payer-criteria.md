## 1. UnitedHealthcare: Surgery of the Knee

**Policy Number:** 2026T0553HH
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2026
**Applies to:** UnitedHealthcare Commercial and Individual Exchange plans

**Source:** [Surgery of the Knee (PDF)](https://www.uhcprovider.com/content/dam/provider/docs/public/policies/comm-medical-drug/surgery-knee.pdf)

### Coverage Rationale

Surgery of the knee is proven and medically necessary in certain circumstances. For medical necessity clinical coverage criteria, UHC refers to **InterQual CP: Procedures** for the following:

- Arthroscopy or Arthroscopically Assisted Surgery, Knee
- Arthroscopy or Arthroscopically Assisted Surgery, Knee (Pediatric)
- Arthroscopy, Diagnostic, +/- Synovial Biopsy, Knee
- Arthrotomy, Knee
- Arthrotomy, Knee (Pediatric)
- Removal and Replacement, Total Joint Replacement (TJR), Knee
- Total Joint Replacement (TJR), Knee
- Unicondylar or Patellofemoral Knee Replacement

**Note:** UHC delegates detailed clinical criteria to **InterQual** (Change Healthcare). The specific criteria (conservative treatment duration, imaging requirements, functional scores) are maintained within the InterQual platform and are not published verbatim in the UHC policy document. Providers must access InterQual directly for point-of-care criteria.

### Key Definitions (from UHC Policy)

- **Disabling Pain:** WOMAC pain domain > 40
- **Functional Disability:** WOMAC functional limitation domain > 40
- **Radiographic Findings of Osteoarthritis:** Narrowing of joint space, osteophyte formation, subchondral sclerosis, subchondral cysts, deformity of bony end-plates, and thinning or complete loss of articular cartilage. UHC will accept **three or more moderate to severe radiographic findings** as sufficient to support diagnosis of severe osteoarthritis.
- **Outerbridge Grades:**
  - Grade 0: Normal
  - Grade I: Cartilage with softening and swelling
  - Grade II: Partial-thickness defect with fissures on the surface that do not reach subchondral bone or exceed 1.5 cm in diameter
  - Grade III: Fissuring to the level of subchondral bone in an area with a diameter more than 1.5 cm
  - Grade IV: Exposed subchondral bone

### Non-Covered / Unproven Procedures

Articular cartilage repair is **unproven and not medically necessary** for any of the following:
- Autologous minced or particulated cartilage (e.g., CAIS, Reveille Cartilage Processor)
- Allogeneic minced or particulated cartilage (e.g., BioCartilage, DeNovo NT Graft, DeNovo ET)
- Collagen Meniscus Implant
- Decellularized Osteochondral Allografts (e.g., Chondrofix)
- Reduced osteochondral discs (e.g., Prochondrix, Cartiform)
- Synthetic Resorbable Polymers (e.g., PolyGraft BGS, TruFit, TruGraft)
- Xenograft implantation

## 2. UnitedHealthcare: Spinal Fusion and Decompression

**Policy Number:** 2026T0639H
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Applies to:** UnitedHealthcare Commercial and Individual Exchange plans

**Source:** [Spinal Fusion and Decompression (PDF)](https://www.uhcprovider.com/content/dam/provider/docs/public/policies/comm-medical-drug/spinal-fusion-decompression.pdf)

### Coverage Rationale

Spinal procedures for the treatment of spine pain are proven and medically necessary in certain circumstances. For medical necessity clinical coverage criteria, UHC refers to **InterQual CP: Procedures**:

- Decompression +/- Fusion, Cervical
- Decompression +/- Fusion, Lumbar
- Decompression +/- Fusion, Thoracic
- Fusion, Cervical Spine
- Fusion, Lumbar Spine
- Fusion, Thoracic Spine
- Scoliosis or Kyphosis Surgery
- Scoliosis or Kyphosis Surgery (Pediatric)

### Non-Covered / Unproven Procedures

The following are **unproven and not medically necessary**:

1. **Isolated Facet Fusion** (with or without instrumentation) -- insufficient evidence of efficacy
2. **Dynamic Stabilization systems** for treatment of degenerative Spondylolisthesis -- insufficient evidence of efficacy
3. **Total Facet Joint Arthroplasty** -- insufficient evidence of efficacy
4. **Dividing treatment of symptomatic, multi-site spinal pathology into serial or Staged Multiple Sessions** when one session can address all sites -- insufficient evidence of safety and efficacy

### Key Definitions (from UHC Policy)

- **Disabling Symptoms:** Oswestry Disability Index score of more than 8, or SF-36 Bodily Pain Score of less than 70, or Physical Function Score of less than 78 (based on Weinstein, 2009 pivotal study)
- **Progressive:** Significant worsening of deficits or symptoms based on at least two measurements over days or weeks (rapidly Progressive) or over months (Progressive) on a validated pain or function scale
- **Radicular Pain:** Pain which radiates from spine into the extremity along the course of the spinal nerve root, following a dermatome pattern. Must include:
  - Positive nerve root tension sign (positive straight leg raise test or femoral tension sign), OR
  - Asymmetric depressed reflex, OR
  - Asymmetric decreased sensation in a dermatomal distribution, OR
  - Asymmetric weakness in a myotomal distribution
  - Imaging should show compression of the corresponding nerve root
- **Spondylolisthesis:** Clinically significant if sagittal plane displacement is at least 3 mm on flexion and extension views or relative sagittal plane angulation greater than 11 degrees (Ghogawala et al, 2016)
- **Unremitting:** Constant and unrelieved by conservative therapy

## 3. UnitedHealthcare: Implanted Electrical Stimulator for the Spinal Cord

**Policy Number:** 2026T0567DD
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Applies to:** UnitedHealthcare Commercial and Individual Exchange plans

**Source:** [Implanted Electrical Stimulator for the Spinal Cord (PDF)](https://www.uhcprovider.com/content/dam/provider/docs/public/policies/comm-medical-drug/implanted-electrical-stimulator-spinal-cord.pdf)

### Coverage Rationale

#### Spinal Cord Stimulation (SCS) -- Covered Indications

Implanted electrical spinal cord stimulators are **proven and medically necessary** for treating the following conditions in certain circumstances when performed according to FDA labeled indications, contraindications, warnings, and precautions:

- **Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS)**
- **Painful diabetic neuropathy**
- **Failed back surgery syndrome**

For medical necessity clinical coverage criteria, refer to InterQual CP: Procedures, Spinal Cord Stimulator (SCS) Insertion.

#### SCS -- Not Covered Indications

Implanted electrical spinal cord stimulators are **unproven and not medically necessary** for:

- **Chronic intractable back pain without prior spine surgery**
- **Refractory angina pectoris**

#### Dorsal Root Ganglion (DRG) Stimulation -- Covered Indications

DRG stimulation is **proven and medically necessary** for:

- **Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS I, CRPS II)**

#### DRG Stimulation -- Not Covered

DRG stimulation is **unproven and not medically necessary** for treating **all other conditions**.

#### Replacement Battery/Generator

Coverage of a replacement battery/generator is appropriate when:
- The individual's existing battery/generator is malfunctioning
- Cannot be repaired
- Is no longer under warranty

### Clinical Practice Guidelines Referenced

- **VA/DoD (2022):** Recommended **against** SCS for patients with low back pain
- **NASS (2020):** No studies adequately address electrical stimulation for low back pain
- **ASRAPM (Shanthanna et al., 2023):** SCS trial should be performed before definitive implantation; all patients should be screened with validated instruments for psychosocial factors including depression
- **NICE (2019):** Supports Senza SCS for chronic neuropathic back or leg pain after failed back surgery, with HF10 therapy, when residual VAS >= 50mm at least 6 months after back surgery

## 4. Cigna/eviCore: Knee Replacement/Arthroplasty (CMM-311)

**Guideline:** CMM-311: Knee Replacement/Arthroplasty
**Version:** V2.0.2025
**Effective Date:** March 7, 2026
**Developed by:** EviCore, Inc. for Cigna

**Source:** [Cigna CMM-311 (PDF)](https://www.evicore.com/sites/default/files/clinical-guidelines/2025-11/Cigna_CMM-311%20Knee%20Replacement%20Arthroplasty_V2.0.2025_Eff03.07.2026_Pub11.21.2025.pdf)

### Definitions

**Kellgren-Lawrence Grading System** (radiographic grading for tibial-femoral joint OA):
- Grade I: doubtful narrowing of joint space and possible osteophytic lipping
- Grade II: definite osteophytes and possible narrowing of joint space
- Grade III: moderate multiple osteophytes, definite narrowing of joint space, some sclerosis, and possible deformity of bone contour
- Grade IV: large osteophytes, marked narrowing of joint space, severe sclerosis, and definite deformity of bone contour

**Non-Surgical Management** includes: relative rest/activity modification; weight loss; supervised physiotherapy modalities and therapeutic exercises; prescription and non-prescription medications; assistive devices; and/or intra-articular injections.

### Partial Knee Replacement -- Medical Necessity Criteria

Partial knee replacement (medial, lateral, or patellofemoral) is considered **medically necessary when ALL of the following criteria have been met:**

1. **Imaging or arthroscopic findings** show EITHER:
   - Severe unicompartmental OA as evidenced by ANY of:
     - Kellgren-Lawrence **Grade IV** radiographic findings
     - Outerbridge Classification **Grade IV** arthroscopic findings AND not a candidate for joint sparing procedure
     - Modified Outerbridge Classification **Grade IV** MRI findings AND not a candidate for joint sparing procedure
   - OR: Unicompartmental avascular necrosis (AVN) of the femoral condyles and/or proximal tibia

2. **Physical exam** demonstrates BOTH:
   - Knee stability
   - Knee arc of motion (full extension to full flexion) >90 degrees

3. **Symptoms** include BOTH:
   - Function-limiting pain at short distances (e.g., walking less than one-quarter mile, limiting activity to two city blocks) for **at least three (3) months duration**
   - Loss of knee function which interferes with age-appropriate ADLs and/or demands of employment

4. **Failure of provider-directed non-surgical management for at least three (3) months duration**
   - Exception: Provider-directed non-surgical management may be inappropriate. The medical record must clearly document why it is not appropriate.
   - Note: It is incumbent on the surgeon to preoperatively optimize reasonably modifiable medical and behavioral health comorbidities.

### Partial Knee Replacement -- Non-Indications (Not Medically Necessary)

- Grade IV patellofemoral joint OA involving lateral patella facet and/or lateral trochlea (when performing medial/lateral unicompartmental)
- Grade III or IV medial/lateral compartment OA (when performing patellofemoral replacement)
- Patellar Malalignment Syndrome (Q angle >15 degrees males, >20 degrees females; TT-TG distance of 20mm)
- Tibial or femoral shaft deformity
- Radiographic evidence of medial or lateral subluxation
- Flexion contracture >15 degrees
- Valgus deformity >20 degrees
- Varus deformity >15 degrees
- Varus deformity >= 10 degrees with sagittal tibiofemoral subluxation >= 6mm (ACL deficient osteoarthritic knee)
- Charcot joint
- Inflammatory arthropathy
- Active local or systemic infection
- Vascular insufficiency (ABI <0.5), significant muscular atrophy, or neuromuscular disease compromising implant stability or post-op recovery

### Total Knee Replacement -- Medical Necessity Criteria

Total knee replacement is considered **medically necessary for ANY of the following conditions when ALL of the associated criteria have been met:**

#### For Osteoarthritis (OA) or Avascular Necrosis (AVN):

1. **Imaging or arthroscopic findings** show EITHER:
   - Severe unicompartmental, bicompartmental, or tricompartmental OA as evidenced by ANY of:
     - Kellgren-Lawrence **Grade III or IV** radiographic findings
     - Outerbridge Classification **Grade IV** arthroscopic findings AND not a candidate for joint sparing procedure
     - Modified Outerbridge Classification **Grade IV** MRI findings AND not a candidate for joint sparing procedure
   - OR: AVN of the femoral condyles and/or proximal tibia

2. **Symptoms** include BOTH:
   - Function-limiting pain at short distances (e.g., walking less than one-quarter mile) for **at least three (3) months duration**
   - Loss of knee function which interferes with age-appropriate ADLs and/or demands of employment

3. **Failure of provider-directed non-surgical management for at least three (3) months duration**
   - Exception: May be inappropriate with documented justification

#### For Fracture of Distal Femur:

- Imaging shows fracture of the distal femur (trochlea, condyles)
- Conservative management or surgical fixation is not considered a reasonable option

### Total Knee Replacement -- Non-Indications (Not Medically Necessary)

- Joint instability (lack of collateral ligament integrity) not amendable to surgical correction
- >30 degrees fixed varus deformity not amendable to surgical correction
- >30 degrees fixed valgus deformity not amendable to surgical correction
- Individuals undergoing dialysis
- Active local or systemic infection
- Vascular insufficiency (ABI <0.5), significant muscular atrophy, or neuromuscular disease

### Experimental/Investigational/Unproven

- Bicompartmental knee arthroplasty (modular or monolithic/nonmodular)
- Bi-unicompartmental knee arthroplasty
- Focal resurfacing of a single knee joint defect (e.g., Arthrosurface)
- Unicompartmental free-floating interpositional device (e.g., UniSpacer)

| CPT Code | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 27438 | Arthroplasty, patella; with prosthesis |
| 27440 | Arthroplasty, knee, tibial plateau |
| 27441 | Arthroplasty, knee, tibial plateau; with debridement and partial synovectomy |
| 27443 | Arthroplasty, femoral condyles or tibial plateau(s), knee; with debridement and partial synovectomy |
| 27445 | Arthroplasty, knee, hinge prosthesis (e.g., Walldius type) |

## 5. Cigna/eviCore: ACL Reconstruction (CMM-312)

**Guideline:** CMM-312: Knee Surgery - Arthroscopic and Open Procedures
**Version:** V1.0.2024
**Effective Date:** August 1, 2024
**Developed by:** EviCore, Inc.

**Source:** [eviCore CMM-312 (PDF)](https://www.evicore.com/sites/default/files/clinical-guidelines/2024-12/eviCore_CMM-312%20Knee%20Surg%20Arthro_Final_V1.0.2024_eff08.01.2024_pub09.17.24_upd12.10.24.pdf)

### ACL Reconstruction -- Medical Necessity Criteria

Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) reconstruction (with allograft or autograft) is considered **medically necessary when ALL of the following criteria have been met:**

1. **Imaging:** MRI, CT arthrogram, or arthroscopy shows a tear/disruption or significant laxity of the ACL

2. **Physical exam** demonstrates ANY of the following:
   - Positive Lachman's test
   - Positive anterior drawer test
   - Positive pivot shift test

3. **Symptoms** include BOTH:
   - Function-limiting knee pain and/or loss of knee function which interferes with ANY of:
     - Ability to carry out age-appropriate ADLs
     - Demands of employment
     - Need to return to activities that require cutting, pivoting, and/or agility in which ACL insufficiency may predispose to further instability episodes that may result in new articular or meniscal cartilage injuries
   - Individual reports **knee instability** (giving way, subjective weakness, or buckling)

4. **Failure of provider-directed non-surgical management for at least three (3) months in duration**
   - **Exception:** Three months of non-surgical management is **NOT required** if there is an acute injury setting and joint instability has been documented with ANY of the following additional conditions:
     - Need to return to activities requiring cutting, pivoting, and/or agility where ACL insufficiency may cause further instability and cartilage injuries
     - A confirmed ACL tear AND a repairable meniscus tear
     - Concomitant ligament injuries (i.e., multi-ligamentous knee injury) that require reconstruction to provide stability

### ACL Reconstruction -- Non-Indications

ACL reconstruction is considered **not medically necessary** for ANY other indication or condition.

### ACL Repair

ACL repair (as distinct from reconstruction) is considered **experimental, investigational, or unproven (EIU)**.

### Key CPT Code

| CPT Code | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 29888 | Arthroscopically aided anterior cruciate ligament repair/augmentation or reconstruction |
| 29889 | Arthroscopically aided posterior cruciate ligament repair/augmentation or reconstruction |

## 6. Cigna/eviCore: Rotator Cuff Repair (CMM-315)

**Guideline:** CMM-315: Shoulder Surgery - Arthroscopic and Open Procedures
**Version:** V2.0.2025
**Effective Date:** March 7, 2026
**Developed by:** EviCore, Inc. for Cigna

**Source:** [Cigna CMM-315 (PDF)](https://www.evicore.com/sites/default/files/clinical-guidelines/2025-11/Cigna_CMM-315%20Shoulder%20Surg%20Arthro%20Open%20Proc_V2.0.2025_Eff03.07.2026_Pub11.21.2025.pdf)

### Rotator Cuff Repair -- Medical Necessity Criteria

Rotator cuff repair is considered **medically necessary when ALL of the following criteria have been met:**

1. **Imaging:** MRI or CT shows EITHER:
   - Grade 2 or 3 partial-thickness rotator cuff tear (Ellman classification)
   - Full-thickness rotator cuff tear (Cofield classification)

2. **Physical exam** demonstrates BOTH (as compared to the non-involved side):
   - EITHER of the following findings:
     - Functionally-limited range of motion
     - Measurable loss of strength of the rotator cuff musculature
   - ANY of the following positive orthopedic tests/signs:
     - Drop Arm Test
     - Painful Arc Test
     - Jobe Test or Empty Can Test
     - External Rotation Lag Sign (Dropping Sign)
     - Internal Rotation Lag Sign
     - Lift-Off Test
     - Bear Hug Test
     - Belly Press Test (Napoleon)
     - Belly-Off Test
     - Neer Impingement Test
     - Hawkins-Kennedy Impingement Test
     - Hornblower Test (Patte)

3. **Symptoms:** Function-limiting pain (e.g., loss of shoulder function which interferes with age-appropriate ADLs and/or demands of employment)

4. **Failure of provider-directed non-surgical management for at least three (3) months duration**
   - **Exception:** Three months of non-surgical management is **NOT required** for an individual who suffers a discrete traumatic event that results in an **acute full-thickness rotator cuff tear** without evidence of a pre-existing chronic rotator cuff tear.
     - The presence of **fatty infiltration and/or muscle atrophy** on MRI or CT is considered evidence of pre-existing chronic rotator cuff tear.
     - Therefore, when fatty infiltration and/or muscle atrophy is present, three months of non-surgical management IS required, regardless of whether a discrete traumatic event occurred.

5. **Other potential pathological conditions have been excluded**, including but not limited to: fracture, thoracic outlet syndrome, brachial plexus disorders, referred neck pain, cervical radiculopathy, and advanced glenohumeral osteoarthritis

### Rotator Cuff Repair -- Non-Indications

Rotator cuff repair is considered **not medically necessary** for ANY other indication or condition.

### Experimental/Investigational/Unproven

- Superior capsular reconstruction using any type of graft (autograft, allograft, xenograft, or synthetic)

## 7. Cigna: Lumbar Spinal Fusion Precertification

**Source:** [Cigna Lumbar Fusion Precertification Form (PDF)](https://static.cigna.com/assets/chcp/pdf/resourceLibrary/medical/cigna-healthcare-lumbar-fusion-precertification-fillable-form.pdf)
**Also see:** [Cigna Cervical Fusion Precertification Form (PDF)](https://static.cigna.com/assets/chcp/pdf/resourceLibrary/medical/cigna-healthcare-cervical-fusion-precertification-fillable-form.pdf)

### Precertification Submission Requirements

Submit to fax: 866-873-8279. The following clinical information must be included:

- Onset of symptoms / chief complaint
- Primary location and distribution of pain
- Symptoms of myelopathy/radiculopathy (weakness, gait imbalance, paresthesias, loss of sensation, bowel or bladder dysfunction)
- Functional limitations (ADLs, inability to perform household chores or prolonged standing, interference with essential job functions, disabilities, neurological deficits)
- Conservative treatment provided and duration (exercise, NSAIDs/steroidal medication unless contraindicated, physical therapy, activity/lifestyle modification)
- Objective physical examination/findings (sensory changes, motor weakness, reflex changes, sustained clonus, Babinski test, toe-to-heel walk, Romberg test, loss of sacral sensation or sphincter tone)
- Reports of all diagnostic studies: X-ray findings, MRI or CT scan findings, CT myelography
- If prior lumbar fusion surgery: when and what levels were previously fused/decompressed

### Tobacco Cessation Requirement

**"I confirm the patient has not smoked or otherwise used tobacco products within the past six weeks."** (Yes/No required)

### Criteria for Single-Level Fusion for Discogenic Low Back Pain (DDD without spondylolisthesis)

If the planned surgery is a **single level fusion for the treatment of discogenic low back pain due to single level degenerative disc disease without a spondylolisthesis**, supporting documentation of EACH of the following is required:

Clinical records that support documentation of **unremitting pain and significant functional impairment for at least 12 months duration**, and during which time **ALL of the following criteria have been met:**

1. **Unremitting pain and significant functional impairment continues despite at least six (6) consecutive months of structured, physician-supervised conservative medical management**, including ALL of the following components:
   - Exercise, including core stabilization exercises
   - Nonsteroidal and/or steroidal medication (unless contraindicated)
   - Physical therapy, including passive and active treatment modalities
   - Activity/lifestyle modification

2. **Participation in 3 or more individual or group cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) sessions** provided by a licensed healthcare professional with competence in principles and practice of CBT (e.g., PT, OT, psychiatrist, psychologist, social worker, psychiatric nurse) providing individualized treatment that includes ALL of the following elements:
   - Disease education
   - Activity and lifestyle modification
   - Stress management (including strategies to deal with emotions such as fear, anxiety, sadness that can interfere with pain management)

3. **Single level degenerative disc disease demonstrated on appropriate imaging studies** (CT, MRI, or discography) as the likely cause of pain

4. **Statement from a primary care physician, neurologist, physiatrist, psychiatrist, psychologist, or other licensed behavioral and/or medical health care provider** attesting to the **absence of untreated, underlying mental health conditions/issues** (e.g., depression, drug abuse, alcohol abuse) as a major contributor to chronic pain

5. **The individual is a nonsmoker**, or in the absence of progressive neurological compromise, **will refrain from use of tobacco products for at least 6 weeks prior to the planned surgery**

### Non-Covered Surgical Techniques/Devices

The following are NOT covered:
- Pre-sacral interbody approach, including axial interbody approach (AxiaLif) [CPT 22586, 0309T, 0195T, 0196T]
- Dynamic spine stabilization device systems (e.g., Dynesys, Stabilimax NZ)
- Total facet arthroplasty, including Total Facet Arthroplasty System [0202T]
- Isolated facet fusion (e.g., TruFUSE, NuFix) [0221T, 0222T]
- Posterior interspinous fixation devices (e.g., Affic, Aspen, Spinous Process Fixation System)

### BMP (Bone Morphogenetic Protein) Coverage

- A single packet of BMP-2 is covered as part of a medically necessary, **single level anterior interbody fusion**
- Use of more than one packet of BMP or BMP for any other lumbar fusion surgery is **generally not covered**
- Osteopromotive cell or factor-based bone graft substitutes (including rhBMP-2 / INFUSE Bone Graft) when used for spinal fusion procedures **other than single-level anterior lumbar or lumbosacral fusion** are considered experimental/investigational/unproven

## 8. Cigna: Carpal Tunnel Release

**Note:** Cigna does not appear to maintain a dedicated eviCore CMM guideline specifically for carpal tunnel release surgery (CPT 64721). Carpal tunnel release is generally covered under standard surgical benefits when medical necessity is established through standard clinical documentation.

### General Medical Necessity Indicators (from Cigna educational resources)

Based on Cigna's published patient education materials, surgery may be considered when:

- Symptoms persist after a long period of nonsurgical treatment (generally not until after several weeks to months of nonsurgical treatment)
- Severe symptoms exist: persistent loss of feeling or coordination in fingers or hand, no strength in thumb
- Damage to the median nerve is confirmed by nerve test results (NCS/EMG) and loss of hand or finger function
- No sign of nerve damage initially, but ongoing symptoms despite conservative management

### Typical Documentation for Authorization

While no formal eviCore clinical guideline exists, standard documentation for carpal tunnel release typically includes:

- **Nerve conduction studies / EMG** confirming median nerve compression at the carpal tunnel
- **Duration of conservative management:** typically 4-12 weeks of splinting, NSAIDs, activity modification, and/or corticosteroid injections
- **Functional limitations** documented in medical record
- **Physical exam findings** consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome (positive Phalen's test, positive Tinel's sign, decreased grip strength, thenar atrophy)

| CPT Code | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 64721 | Neuroplasty and/or transposition; median nerve at carpal tunnel |
